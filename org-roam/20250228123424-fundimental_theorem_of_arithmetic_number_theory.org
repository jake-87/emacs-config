#+title:Fundimental theorem of arithmetic, number theory

Every number $n > 1$ can be written as a unique prime factorization, for primes $p_0, \dots, p_n$ and powers $a_0, \dots, a_n$:

\[n = p_0^{a_0} \times p_1^{a_1} + \dots + p_n^{a_n}\].

with $p_0 < p_1 < \dots < p_n$, and $a_0, \dots, a_n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$.
