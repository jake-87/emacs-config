#+title:Pinch theorem, analysis

The pinch theorem states that for three functions $f,g,h : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$, where:

\[\forall x, f(x) \leq g(x) \leq h(x)\]

if $\lim_{x\to k} f(x) = \lim_{x \to k} h(x) = L$, then $\lim_{x \to k} g(x) = L$. In other words, the limit is "pinched" between the other two functions.
