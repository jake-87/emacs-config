#+title:Colinear, Algebra

Points are co-linear if they lie on the same line.
In other words, there exists a set of $\lambda$s s.t. on some given line, $L(\lambda_1) = A$, $L(\lambda_2) = B$, $L(\lambda_3) = C$.
This has the consequence of meaning that $AB$ and $BC$ are parallel.
