#+title:L'hopital's rule, calculus


If $f,g$ are differentiable in the neighbourhood of some $a \in \mathbb{R$.

If:

- $f(x) \to 0$ and $g(x) \to 0$ as $x \to a$, or

- $f(x) \to \infty$ and $g(x) to \infty$ as $x \to a$


then:

\[\lim_{x\to a}} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}} = \lim_{x\to a}} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}}\]

assuming the limit exists.
